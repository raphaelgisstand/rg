Instructional Design

Instructional design is the framework of how educators construct learning environments and solve real world challenges of meeting student needs. The ADDIE model of instructional design consists of five stages in the process: Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation and Evaluation. By implementing technology instruction, teachers can successfully apply the ADDIE model to strengthen critical thinking and influence both metacognition and self-regulation in their students. Instructional design has been constructed as a process to determine the causes of skill and learning deficiencies when dealing with student learning. By applying the ADDIE model in classroom technology instructional strategies, teachers can assess student learning by implementing an instructional design model to address the needs assessment. By using instructional design, educators can address the student learning deficiencies by providing differentiated instruction and incorporating student centered technology to bridge achievement gaps.

Instructional Design Project

For the course Instructional Design, As a group, we were able to help Dr. Lenhart solve her dilemma by creating a user-friendly, online course via a learning management system. The process of creating an instructional design model (IDM) was facilitated by the application of the ADDIE model (Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, and Evaluation) to assist in the solving of a real-world problem. The task of the group was to design an IDM in a group setting for a graduate, online course at the University of Akron.

 

ID Project Group Paper

For the course Instructional Design, As a group, we constructed the final paper to our ID Project. The group paper broke down and explained each part of the ADDIE Model and the role they played in the instructional design process. Our task was to explain in the paper the steps it took for us to create these 8 online modules for Dr. Lenhart. The group process was rewarding as it taught the importance of learning to work as a team and manage time schedules.
