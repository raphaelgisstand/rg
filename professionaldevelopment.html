Professional Development



Professional Development is preparation for educators and is necessary to equip them with the tools to teach and adapt to the changing landscape of education. Having knowledge of how students learn and the ways in which they learn best, educators can elect the appropriate lesson and incorporate the necessary technology tools. With differing levels of prior knowledge that students bring to the classroom, assessing student achievement levels can be useful for teachers in determining the most efficient instructional strategy that applies to all learners. Professional development is the preparation for teachers beyond the classroom setting. In my graduate student teaching field experiences at Inventor’s Hall of Fame, Garfield High School and Litchfield Middle School, I gained professional development working with adolescent to young adults. In my experience and participation with the LeBron James Family Foundation, I was trained in working with at-risk youth. While working at Project Learn, I was introduced to interacting with adult learners seeking their General Equivalency Diploma. The artifacts will illustrate the application of the knowledge that I learned while completing my Master degrees.

Technology Philosophy

For the course Intro. to Instructional Technology, I developed a Philosophy of Instructional Technology explaining how I use 21st century technology tools for teaching and learning in the classroom. Included in my Technology Philosophy is a detailed description of my method of practicing ethical uses of technology in the classroom. As a technology leader, I will also explain how my Technology Philosophy aligns with TPACK and 21st Century learning.

Smartboards

For the course Integrating and Implementing Technology, I had to create a video showing my classmates how they can use and implement technology tools and programs into their lessons to teach their students. The technology tool that I educated my peers on was digital whiteboards (smartboards). So, in other words, this was a professional development opportunity for my classmates.
