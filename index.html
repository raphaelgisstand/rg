Technology Vision Statement

In the 21st century, technology has become an increasingly useful tool for the field of Education. For educators, finding instructional strategies that influence student learning has been challenging due to the changes in state content standards and the different levels of student proficiency. Williamson and Radish (2009) in Technology Facilitation and Leadership Standards state “using technology in the classroom affects the learning environment and culture...the mere presence of technology alters space arrangements and the flow of activities.” Implementation of technology in classroom lessons engages students. The advent of technology in the classroom strengthens classroom instruction for teachers and the learning process for students. The wide array of technological resources available to teachers can complement any subject area curriculum. Online based module learning activities, subject based computer lessons, educational games, and learning software applications are available as resource tools for teachers and parents. With the addition of digital components, instructional lesson plans allow for students to interact with the knowledge being presented. Technology based instruction allows educators to become more flexible in classroom instruction by blending technology tools and content area standards. Technology in the classroom can have a positive impact on student learning by offering interactive tools that can act as self-monitoring, tutorial-based lessons. The International Society for Technology in Education’s development of the National Educational Technology Standards for Teachers (NETS-T) prepares teachers to adjust classroom instruction to meet the changing landscape of education and the digital age of technology. Technology activities offer innovative approaches for instructional lessons that teachers can use to increase student interests while achieving state content standards.
